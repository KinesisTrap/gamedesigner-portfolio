Game Mechanics

Three original subsystems designed, documented and balanced as part of the same board-game & prototype ecosystem.

Lockpicking System

Playtest Ready · v0.1

Allows players to gain access to locked doors, chests, safes and archives during exploration and heist scenarios. Designed to create tension during infiltration, reward investment into the Lockpicking skill, and avoid hard-locking the game on failed rolls.

Core loop: each lock is a set of colored D6 “pins” with a revealed target combination. Matching a die to its target fixes that pin; unresolved dice are re-rolled together with Modifier Dice earned from the character’s skill level, which can nudge, re-roll, or directly fix pins.

DifficultyPinsRecommended Noise Threshold
Easy32–3
Medium42
Hard51–2

Failed attempts accumulate local Lock Pressure; once it crosses the lock’s Noise Threshold, it converts into global Scene Noise — driving stealth consequences (patrols, guard arrival, alarms) without ever fully blocking progress. Balanced with Monte Carlo simulations across skill levels.

Social Encounter System

Design Draft
The NPC presents a sequence of social defenses based on their personality. A player responds with cards from their Diplomacy Deck. The group tries to gain enough Influence before the conversation reaches its Tension limit.

Each NPC has a Character Profile built from three trait pairs (Legality, Temperament, Generosity), which assembles a 15-card Diplomacy Defense Deck from six underlying Defense Decks. Players respond using cards from six Diplomacy Types — Seal, Blade, Heart, Mask, Coin, Freedom.

Round structure: gain Tension → draw 3 cards → reveal 1 NPC Defense Card → play response cards → resolve Influence/Tension → discard & repeat. Tension reaching the NPC’s limit fails the encounter immediately, even if an Influence target was reached the same round.

TrackRangeEffect
Influence-2 to 10Progress toward the conversation goal
Tension0 to 10Reaching the NPC’s limit fails the encounter

Design intent: NPC personality drives defense style rather than a flat difficulty number — Lawful NPCs escalate Tension formally, Outlaw NPCs disrupt structure and reduce Influence, and future traits (Prideful, Cowardly, Greedy, Generous) each get a distinct pressure pattern.

Arcane Duel — Card Duel Mechanic

Playable Vertical Slice

A fast, decision-dense deckbuilding × CCG hybrid: two archmages duel by channelling Arcane Power into spells, creatures and artifacts, upgrading a personal deck and overwhelming the opponent. A single match lasts roughly 10–15 minutes.

  • •  Two resources — per-turn Arcane (resets each turn) vs. bankable Essence, creating a tempo-vs-investment tension.
  • •  Two identities — Fire (aggression and reach) vs. Water (sustain and resilience).
  • •  Free play, paid acquisition — cards in hand are free to play; Arcane and Essence are spent to buy new cards into the deck.
  • •  Automatic, deterministic combat and low variance — randomness limited to deck shuffles and market order.

Shipped as a playable vertical slice with an engine-agnostic, data-driven rules core (TypeScript) decoupled from its React/Vite presentation layer, so it can migrate to a commercial engine without a rewrite.
